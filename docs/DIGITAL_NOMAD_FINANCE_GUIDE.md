# Digital Nomad Financial Guide by Country (2025-2026)

> Practical financial information for digital nomads spending 1-6 months per country. Last updated: April 2026.

---

# SOUTHEAST ASIA

---

## Thailand

### Banking
- **Best banks**: Kasikorn Bank (KBank) has the best mobile app with QR/PromptPay integration. Bangkok Bank has the largest branch/ATM network. SCB Easy app is also popular with nomads.
- **Account opening**: As of January 2026, Bangkok Bank requires a **long-term visa** (Non-B, Non-O, ED, LTR, or retirement extension). Tourist visa and DTV holders **can no longer open accounts**. You need a Thai phone number with active SIM for mobile banking.
- **Tip**: Choose a branch familiar with foreign customers (e.g., Silom, Asoke areas in Bangkok). Go early to avoid queues.

### Payment Apps
- **PromptPay**: Thailand's national instant payment system. QR code payments accepted almost everywhere -- restaurants, markets, utilities, even government services. Linked to your Thai bank account.
- Nearly all daily transactions in Bangkok can be done via PromptPay QR codes.

### Currency Restrictions
- THB is relatively freely convertible for everyday amounts.
- No major restrictions on bringing money in for personal use, but amounts over $20,000 equivalent must be declared.
- Transferring large sums out requires documentation of the source.

### Crypto Regulation
- **5-year tax exemption (2025-2029)**: Under Ministerial Regulation No. 399, profits from selling crypto through Thai SEC-licensed exchanges are **tax-free** from Jan 2025 to Dec 2029.
- Exemption applies only to capital gains, not staking/mining/airdrops.
- Exchanges are regulated by the Thai SEC. Must be licensed and KYC-compliant.
- Gains still need to be reported in tax returns even though exempt.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- **180-day rule**: If you stay under 180 days in a calendar year, you are a non-resident taxed **only on Thai-sourced income**. Foreign income is not taxed.
- If you stay 180+ days: Foreign income remitted to Thailand is taxable as of Jan 1, 2024. Progressive rates up to 35%.
- **LTR visa holders**: Exempt from tax on foreign-sourced income entirely.
- A proposed exemption on remitted foreign income for 2026+ is being discussed but **not yet enacted**.

### Moving Money
- **Wise**: Has temporarily stopped issuing THB account details. You can still send TO Thailand via Wise.
- **Bangkok Bank**: Has a New York branch, useful for USD transfers or US Social Security deposits.
- ATM withdrawals: 220 THB fee per transaction at most ATMs, 20,000 THB max per withdrawal.

### Fintech Ecosystem
- SCB Easy, K PLUS (KBank), and Bangkok Bank Mobile are the main banking apps.
- PromptPay is the dominant payment rail.
- Rabbit LINE Pay and TrueMoney Wallet are also used locally.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Bangkok $1,000-1,800/month; Chiang Mai $700-1,200/month.
- **Pitfall**: Opening a bank account on a tourist visa is now essentially impossible. Plan ahead if you need local banking.
- Cash is still useful in markets and smaller towns outside Bangkok.
- PromptPay QR has made Thailand largely cashless in urban areas.

## Vietnam

### Banking
- **Best banks**: Vietcombank, BIDV, Techcombank, and MB Bank are most foreigner-friendly with English-language service.
- **Account opening**: State Bank regulations require **12+ months residence validity**. Tourist visa holders generally cannot open accounts. Foreign teachers/workers with valid work permits can open accounts at all major banks.
- Required: Passport, valid visa with 12+ months validity, local phone number.

### Payment Apps
- **MoMo**: Vietnam's dominant e-wallet with 434M+ in funding. QR codes widely used. However, e-wallets must be denominated in VND, and monthly transaction limit is **100 million VND** (~$4,000).
- **ZaloPay** and **VNPay** are also widely used.
- Foreign-linked accounts are limited; most wallets require a Vietnamese bank account.

### Currency Restrictions
- **VND is a restricted currency** -- not freely convertible outside Vietnam.
- All international transfers require documented evidence of legitimate fund sources.
- Cash: Can bring in unlimited foreign currency but must declare amounts over $5,000 equivalent. Taking VND out of the country is restricted.

### Crypto Regulation
- **Landmark change**: Vietnam's Law on Digital Technology Industry (June 2025, effective Jan 1, 2026) officially recognizes crypto as legal property.
- Government Resolution 05/2025 establishes a 5-year pilot programme (2025-2030) for crypto trading.
- Draft Circular (Feb 2026): Crypto transfers will use securities-like tax treatment. Not subject to VAT. Foreign enterprises pay 0.1% on gross proceeds.
- All crypto trading must be done in VND through Vietnamese-incorporated companies.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-residents taxed only on Vietnam-sourced income at a flat rate of 20%.
- Tax residency: Present 183+ days in a year OR have a permanent residence.
- Withholding tax on contractor payments: 5% for personal income, 1-5% for corporate income depending on sector.

### Moving Money
- Wise supports sending to Vietnam but options are limited for sending FROM Vietnam.
- Remittance services like Western Union, MoneyGram available.
- Bank wire transfers for documented purposes. All require proof of fund source.

### Fintech Ecosystem
- MoMo is the main super-app (payments, insurance, lending, investments).
- VNPay handles most QR code payments at merchants.
- Timo is Vietnam's neobank (partnership with VPBank).

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Ho Chi Minh City $800-1,400/month; Hanoi $700-1,200/month; Da Nang $600-1,000/month.
- **Pitfall**: VND is restricted -- plan your exit strategy for funds. Converting back to USD/EUR at departure can be bureaucratic.
- Cash remains important outside major cities.
- ATM fees: 22,000-55,000 VND per withdrawal; max withdrawal typically 2-3M VND.

## Indonesia / Bali

### Banking
- **Best banks**: BCA (Bank Central Asia) is the most popular with both locals and foreigners -- modern ATMs, low fees, excellent mobile banking. Mandiri and BNI are also solid.
- **Account opening**: Unlike Thailand, you do **not** need a KITAS to open an account. Tourist visas (VoA), social visas, and D1 digital nomad visas all work. Typically requires 10-20 million IDR initial deposit (~$600-1,200) depending on branch.
- Documents: Passport, visa, proof of address, local phone number. Process often completed in under an hour.

### Payment Apps
- **GoPay** (Gojek), **OVO**, **DANA**, **ShopeePay** are the big four.
- Require a local phone number and usually a local bank account.
- QRIS (Quick Response Code Indonesian Standard) is the unified QR payment system accepted almost everywhere.

### Currency Restrictions
- IDR is relatively controlled. Foreign currency transactions over $25,000 equivalent require documentation.
- No major restrictions on bringing cash in/out but must declare amounts over $10,000 equivalent.
- Bank Indonesia requires underlying documentation for outward transfers exceeding certain thresholds.

### Crypto Regulation
- **Crypto oversight shifted from Bappebti to OJK** (Financial Services Authority).
- MOF Regulation No. 50/2025 (effective Aug 1, 2025): Final income tax on crypto transactions is **0.21%** through domestic exchanges, **1%** through foreign platforms.
- Crypto transfers are **VAT-exempt**, but platforms facilitating trades pay 2.2% VAT.
- Mining income taxed under normal income tax from 2026.
- Foreign tax credits **cannot** be applied against Indonesian crypto income tax.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-residents taxed only on Indonesian-sourced income at a flat **20% withholding tax**.
- Tax residency: Present 183+ days in a 12-month period.
- **Digital nomad visa (E33G)**: Valid 1 year, renewable. Income earned from foreign employers is generally not subject to Indonesian tax, but this should be confirmed with a tax advisor.

### Moving Money
- Wise supports IDR transfers. Revolut also works well with Indonesian ATMs.
- Bank transfers via SWIFT from international banks to BCA/Mandiri.
- ATM fees: ~5,000-7,500 IDR per foreign card withdrawal; max 1.25-2.5M IDR per transaction.

### Fintech Ecosystem
- GoPay/Gojek is a super-app (rides, food, payments, lending).
- OVO (backed by Grab) and DANA are widely used.
- Flip is popular for fee-free domestic bank transfers.
- Jago and Bank Neo Commerce are emerging digital banks.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Bali (Canggu/Ubud) $1,000-2,000/month; Jakarta $800-1,500/month.
- **Pitfall**: ATM skimming is still common in Bali -- use machines inside banks.
- Bali's digital nomad visa makes it one of the easiest SEA destinations for banking access.
- QRIS QR codes make Bali surprisingly cashless in tourist areas.

## Malaysia

### Banking
- **Best banks**: Maybank (largest, broadest network), CIMB, Public Bank, and HSBC Malaysia for international clients.
- **Account opening**: Requires a valid long-term visa (employment, student, MM2H). Tourist visa holders typically cannot open accounts. Documents: Passport, visa, proof of address, Malaysian phone number.
- Maybank offers dedicated relationship managers for foreign HNW clients.

### Payment Apps
- **Touch 'n Go eWallet**: The dominant digital wallet, used for tolls, transit, parking, and merchant payments.
- **GrabPay Malaysia**, **Boost**, and **MAE by Maybank** are also popular.
- DuitNow QR is Malaysia's national QR payment standard.

### Currency Restrictions
- MYR is a managed float currency. Non-residents can maintain foreign currency accounts freely.
- No restrictions on bringing foreign currency in. Taking out more than RM10,000 equivalent must be declared.
- Non-residents can freely remit funds out of Malaysia from foreign currency accounts.

### Crypto Regulation
- Crypto exchanges must be registered with Securities Commission (SC). Major licensed exchanges: Luno, Tokenize, MX Global.
- **Tax treatment**: Investment gains (long-term holds) are **not taxed**. Active trading income (revenue gains) is taxable as regular income up to 30%.
- SC amended digital asset regulations in Jan 2025, expanding classification.
- Digital Ringgit (CBDC) being researched via Project Dunbar.

### MM2H Financial Requirements (2025-2026)
The programme has 4 tiers:
- **Silver**: RM500,000 fixed deposit + RM600,000+ property purchase
- **Gold**: RM2 million fixed deposit + RM1 million+ property
- **Platinum/SEZ tiers**: Higher requirements
- Maybank USD fixed deposits yield 4.5-5.2% p.a. in 2025-2026.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-residents taxed at a **flat 30%** on Malaysian-sourced income (vs. progressive rates for residents).
- Foreign-sourced income is **not taxed** for individuals (exemption extended through 2026).
- Tax residency: Present 182+ days in a calendar year.
- No capital gains tax (except on real property and shares in real property companies).

### Moving Money
- Wise card is available to personal customers in Malaysia.
- SWIFT transfers through major banks work well.
- Instarem and Remitly also popular for remittances.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Kuala Lumpur $800-1,500/month; Penang $600-1,100/month.
- **Pitfall**: No specific digital nomad visa. Most nomads use social visit passes (90 days) but cannot legally work. DE Rantau pass is available for some tech workers.
- Touch 'n Go is essential for daily life, especially for highway tolls.

## Philippines

### Banking
- **Best banks**: BDO Unibank (largest), BPI (Bank of the Philippine Islands), Metrobank, Security Bank, UnionBank.
- **Account opening**: Foreigners can open accounts, especially in business districts. Branches in Makati (Salcedo, Legazpi, Ayala), BGC, and Ortigas are most experienced with foreign clients.
- Required: Passport, valid visa (ACR-I card for long-term), proof of address, TIN (Tax Identification Number).

### Payment Apps
- **GCash**: Dominant e-wallet (Globe Telecom). Partnerships with Ria Money Transfer and Viamericas for international remittances from 160+ countries.
- **Maya** (formerly PayMaya): Strong competitor, partnered with TerraPay. Google Pay now supports tap-to-pay with GCash, Maya, and GoTyme.
- As of Feb 2025, GCash/Maya loading from BPI uses Instapay/PESOnet interbank transfer.

### Currency Restrictions
- PHP is freely convertible for reasonable amounts.
- BSP allows purchase of up to $60,000 equivalent per year without documentation.
- Bringing cash in: Must declare amounts over $10,000 or PHP 50,000.

### Crypto Regulation
- Dual regulatory framework: BSP (for remittance/exchange operations) + SEC (for Crypto Asset Service Providers).
- SEC requires minimum paid-up capital of PHP 100 million for CASPs.
- **Tax**: 15% capital gains tax on crypto sales + 12% VAT implemented.
- Philippines adopting OECD Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF) by 2028.
- 12.79 million crypto users projected by 2026.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-resident aliens engaged in trade/business: 0-35% progressive rates on Philippine-sourced income.
- Non-resident aliens not engaged in trade/business: Flat 25% on gross income.
- Tax residency: Generally based on intent and circumstances, not just days. 180+ days creates a presumption.
- Withholding tax on compensation: 0-35% progressive.

### Moving Money
- **Remittance hub**: $38.34 billion in remittances in 2024 (3% YoY growth).
- GCash-Viamericas partnership enables real-time transfers from US/Canada.
- Wise, Remitly, Xoom all support PHP transfers.
- BDO Kabayan Savings offers free transfers between BDO accounts.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Manila (Makati/BGC) $800-1,500/month; Cebu $600-1,000/month; Siargao $500-900/month.
- **Pitfall**: Internet speeds can be inconsistent outside Metro Manila.
- GCash is practically essential -- even sari-sari stores accept it.
- The Philippines has the strongest remittance infrastructure in SEA.

## Cambodia

### Banking
- **Best banks**: ABA Bank (most popular with expats, best mobile app), Acleda Bank (largest local bank), Canadia Bank, Wing (for mobile money).
- **Account opening**: Very easy. Just passport and visa is often enough. USD and KHR accounts usually opened together.
- ABA Bank offers USD fixed deposits yielding up to **8% p.a.** -- among the highest USD deposit rates in the region.

### Payment Apps
- **Bakong**: The National Bank of Cambodia's blockchain-based payment platform. Unifies different banking apps for QR payments. Works for inter-bank transfers.
- ABA Bank, Acleda, and Wing apps all support QR payments -- accepted almost everywhere from tuk-tuks to convenience stores.

### Currency Restrictions
- **De facto dollarized economy**: USD is widely used and accepted everywhere. KHR (Khmer Riel) used mainly for small change.
- No significant capital controls on USD.
- No restrictions on bringing money in or out for personal amounts.
- The government encourages KHR usage but USD dominance continues.

### Crypto Regulation
- NBC issued cryptoasset regulation in 2024, with detailed CASP licensing rules expected in follow-up Prakas.
- Crypto is in a regulatory grey zone -- not explicitly banned, not fully regulated yet.
- Cannot use crypto to pay taxes (must use KHR or approved fiat).
- Cambodia has a reputation as crypto-friendly, but formal regulation is still evolving.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Tax residency: Present 182+ days in a calendar year or have a principal place of abode in Cambodia.
- Non-residents: 14% flat tax on Cambodian-sourced salary income.
- No personal income tax on foreign-sourced income.
- No capital gains tax framework currently (being discussed).
- Tax on interest income: 14% for non-residents.

### Moving Money
- USD-based economy makes international transfers simple.
- SWIFT transfers to ABA/Acleda work well.
- Wing money is the dominant mobile money transfer service.
- Wise can send to Cambodia.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Phnom Penh $600-1,200/month; Siem Reap $400-800/month.
- **Major advantage**: USD economy means no currency conversion risk.
- **Pitfall**: High USD fixed deposit rates reflect higher country risk -- don't put all savings here.
- Banking is remarkably easy compared to most Asian countries.
- ATMs dispense USD.

## India

### Banking
- **NRO/NRE accounts**: Foreigners of Indian origin can open NRE (Non-Resident External) or NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) accounts. Non-Indian foreigners have very limited options.
- **For non-Indian foreigners**: Can open accounts only with valid visa + FRRO registration. Process is bureaucratic and varies heavily by branch.
- Major banks: SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis Bank.

### Payment Apps
- **UPI ecosystem**: PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm are the big three.
- **NRIs from 12 countries** (USA, UK, UAE, Australia, Singapore, Canada, etc.) can now use UPI with foreign mobile numbers linked to NRE/NRO accounts.
- Non-Indian foreigners: Very limited access. Some tourist UPI solutions emerging but not widespread.
- Daily UPI limits: Usually around 1 lakh INR (~$1,200) per day.

### Currency Restrictions
- **INR is not fully convertible**. Strict RBI controls on foreign exchange.
- Can bring in unlimited foreign currency but must declare amounts over $5,000 (cash) or $10,000 (cash + instruments).
- Taking INR out: Limited to 25,000 INR.
- Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS): Indian residents can remit up to $250,000/year. Does not apply to non-residents.

### Crypto Regulation
- **30% flat tax** on all crypto gains. No deductions allowed except cost of acquisition.
- **1% TDS** on all crypto transfers (deducted at source).
- No loss offsetting -- losses from crypto cannot reduce gains from other crypto or any other income.
- 2026 budget adds penalty framework for missed crypto reporting.
- Exchanges: WazirX, CoinDCX, CoinSwitch available domestically.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- **182-day rule**: Present 182+ days = tax resident (taxed on worldwide income).
- **120-day rule**: If Indian-source income exceeds 15 lakh INR (~$18,000), threshold drops to 120 days for RNOR status.
- Non-residents taxed only on Indian-sourced income at slab rates (up to 30% + surcharges).
- India has extensive tax treaty network (90+ countries).
- TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) applies to most payments to non-residents.

### Moving Money
- Wise supports INR transfers (sending to India is easy; from India is limited).
- SWIFT transfers through major banks.
- Western Union, MoneyGram widely available.
- UAE Exchange and other remittance corridors are strong.

### Fintech Ecosystem
- UPI processed 16+ billion transactions/month by 2025.
- PhonePe (Walmart-backed) dominates with 48%+ UPI market share.
- Paytm faced regulatory issues in 2024 but continues operating.
- Razorpay, Pine Labs for merchant payments.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Bengaluru $600-1,200/month; Goa $500-1,000/month; Delhi $500-1,000/month.
- **Major pitfall**: Banking access is extremely limited for non-Indian foreigners. Bring enough cash or use international cards.
- UPI has made India nearly cashless in cities, but foreigners are largely locked out.
- e-Visa holders have even fewer banking options.
- ATM withdrawals: 10,000-20,000 INR max per transaction; fees vary.

## Sri Lanka

### Banking
- **Major banks**: Commercial Bank of Ceylon, Bank of Ceylon, Seylan Bank, Hatton National Bank.
- **Non-resident accounts (CTRA)**: Persons residing overseas can open Sri Lankan Rupee accounts (Capital Transaction Rupee Account) to make payments in Sri Lanka or deposit sale proceeds of local assets.
- Foreigners need passport, visa, and proof of funds source.

### Payment Apps
- Limited digital payment ecosystem compared to other Asian countries.
- LankaPay is the national payment switch.
- Bank apps (Commercial Bank, HNB) offer mobile banking but adoption is lower than in SEA.

### Currency Restrictions
- **LKR is a restricted currency** with a history of forex crisis.
- Strict controls: All currency hedging must have genuine commercial basis.
- Residents can only send limited amounts abroad through Personal Foreign Currency Accounts (up to USD 20,000).
- Foreigners should bring USD/EUR cash and convert locally.
- Post-crisis recovery: Central bank bought $1.4 billion from commercial banks in Jan-Sep 2025, indicating improving reserves.

### Crypto Regulation
- No specific crypto legislation. CBSL has issued warnings but no outright ban.
- Very limited crypto exchange infrastructure locally.
- Not recommended as a base for crypto activities.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-residents taxed on Sri Lanka-sourced income.
- Tax residency: Present 183+ days.
- Withholding tax on interest paid to non-residents: 5%.
- No capital gains tax for non-residents on most assets (some exceptions for real property).

### Moving Money
- Remittances are critical to the economy but infrastructure is recovering from the 2022 crisis.
- SWIFT transfers through major banks.
- Informal channels (hawala) are discouraged but exist.
- Wise supports transfers to Sri Lanka.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Colombo $500-1,000/month; Southern coast $400-800/month.
- **Major pitfall**: Post-crisis foreign currency scarcity means ATMs may have withdrawal limits. Bring USD cash.
- Exchange rates have stabilized but remain volatile.
- Infrastructure improvements ongoing but less developed than SEA neighbors.

## Nepal

### Banking
- **Major banks**: Nepal Rastra Bank (central bank), Nabil Bank, Standard Chartered Nepal, NIC Asia.
- **Foreigners**: Very limited banking access. Non-residents generally cannot open accounts for investment purposes.
- Tourist accounts possible at some banks for basic services with passport and visa.

### Payment Apps
- eSewa and Khalti are the main digital wallets, but primarily for Nepali citizens.
- Foreign tourists have very limited access to local payment apps.
- Cash remains dominant.

### Currency Restrictions
- **NPR is a restricted currency**, pegged to INR at 1.6:1.
- Cash limits: Can bring/take up to **USD 5,000** equivalent. Amounts above must be declared.
- Foreign investment abroad by Nepali companies requires NRB approval.
- **FATF Grey List** (since Feb 2025): Causing slower banking transactions, costlier transfers, and complications in remittances.

### Crypto Regulation
- Nepal Rastra Bank has **banned** cryptocurrency trading, mining, and transactions.
- Violation can lead to legal prosecution.
- No licensed exchanges or legal framework for crypto.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-residents taxed on Nepal-sourced income.
- Tax residency: Present 183+ days.
- Withholding tax: 15-25% depending on payment type.
- No tax treaties with most Western countries.

### Moving Money
- Daily inbound remittance limit raised to **NPR 2.5 million** (Oct 2025).
- Western Union, IME, Prabhu Money Transfer are major remittance channels.
- SWIFT transfers available but slow and expensive due to FATF grey list status.
- Wise does not directly support NPR.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Kathmandu $400-800/month; Pokhara $300-600/month.
- **Major pitfall**: FATF grey listing is making international banking increasingly difficult.
- Bring USD cash. ATMs have low withdrawal limits (max 35,000-50,000 NPR per transaction).
- Internet can be unreliable outside Kathmandu.

## China (Mainland)

### Banking
- **Major banks**: ICBC, Bank of China, China Construction Bank, Agricultural Bank of China.
- **Account opening for foreigners**: Requires passport, valid visa (Z-work visa preferred), local phone number, and proof of address. Tourist visa holders face difficulty.
- **Practical reality**: Even with an account, many features are restricted for foreigners.

### Payment Apps
- **WeChat Pay**: Now accepts foreign cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, JCB, Diners, Discover). Limits: **RMB 6,000/transaction**, **RMB 50,000/month**, **RMB 60,000/year** (~$8,347).
- **Alipay**: Also accepts foreign cards. Limit: **RMB 3,000/transaction** (lower than WeChat). Annual limits similar.
- Both apps are essential -- China is virtually cashless in urban areas.
- **UnionPay**: China's domestic card network, accepted everywhere.

### Currency Restrictions
- **$50,000 annual conversion quota** for individuals (both Chinese citizens and foreigners).
- Financial institutions must report: domestic cash transactions over 50,000 RMB (~$7,350), all cross-border transactions over $10,000.
- Amounts exceeding $50,000 require a SAFE (State Administration of Foreign Exchange) permit.
- Strict capital controls on moving money out of China.

### Crypto Regulation
- **Complete ban**: All crypto-related activities are illegal since 2021, reinforced in 2025 and expanded in February 2026.
- Feb 2026 expansion: Explicitly bans real-world asset (RWA) tokenization and unapproved offshore yuan-pegged stablecoins.
- Owning crypto privately is in a grey zone, but all trading, mining, and exchange operations are illegal.
- Penalties are enforced and increasing.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Tax residency: Present 183+ days.
- Non-residents: Taxed on China-sourced income at progressive rates up to 45%.
- China has a "six-year rule" for foreign residents -- exempt from worldwide taxation if you leave China for 30+ consecutive days at least once in any rolling 6-year period.
- Extensive tax treaty network (100+ countries).

### Moving Money
- **VPN needed** for accessing foreign banking apps and websites.
- Foreign bank accounts can receive RMB but outward transfers face strict documentation requirements.
- SWIFT transfers from Chinese banks are slow and bureaucratic.
- Wise has limited functionality in China.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Beijing/Shanghai $1,200-2,500/month; Chengdu/Kunming $700-1,200/month.
- **Critical pitfall**: Without WeChat Pay/Alipay, daily life is extremely difficult. Set up before arrival.
- Foreign card integration with WeChat/Alipay has low limits -- bring enough for your stay.
- VPN is essential for accessing Gmail, Google, WhatsApp, most Western services.
- Cash is increasingly refused in some places.

## Hong Kong

### Banking
- **Best banks**: HSBC (most widely used), Standard Chartered, Hang Seng Bank, Bank of China (Hong Kong).
- **Account opening**: Foreigners can open accounts but most traditional banks require **in-person interview**. HKMA has relaxed policies for non-residents in 2025, with more virtual banks participating.
- Documents: Passport, valid visa/entry permit, proof of address (last 3 months).
- **Virtual banks**: ZA Bank, Mox, WeLab Bank, Airstar Bank offer easier online account opening.
- Fees: Standard application fee ~HKD 1,300 online.

### Payment Apps
- **Octopus Card**: Contactless smart card accepted for MTR, buses, ferries, trams, convenience stores, restaurants, vending machines -- essentially everywhere. Available as physical card or phone app.
- **FPS (Faster Payment System)**: HK's instant payment system supporting HKD and RMB.
- AlipayHK and WeChat Pay HK also available.

### Currency Restrictions
- **HKD pegged to USD** (7.75-7.85 range) -- effectively no currency risk for USD holders.
- Very liberal capital flow regime. No capital controls.
- No restrictions on bringing money in or out.
- One of the world's freest economies for financial transactions.

### Crypto Regulation
- **Regulated crypto hub**: Multi-layered framework using AMLO and Securities and Futures Ordinance.
- HSBC and Standard Chartered approved as first stablecoin issuers (March 2026).
- Licensed exchanges operating under HKMA/SFC oversight.
- Stablecoins Ordinance passed May 2025, effective Aug 2025: Full backing required, T+1 redemption at face value.
- HKMA encourages banks to support crypto sector clients.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- **Territorial taxation**: Only income sourced in HK is taxed, regardless of nationality.
- Progressive rates up to 17%; standard rate 15% (up to HK$5M), 16% thereafter.
- **60-day rule**: Non-residents with employment visits totaling 60 days or less are exempt from salaries tax.
- No capital gains tax, no VAT/GST, no withholding tax on dividends.
- No tax on foreign-sourced income.

### Moving Money
- Extremely easy. SWIFT, FPS, and international transfers all function smoothly.
- Wise supports HKD fully.
- No restrictions on inward/outward remittances.
- HKD/USD peg means minimal currency conversion costs.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Very high. $2,500-5,000+/month depending on housing.
- **Major advantage**: Tax-friendly jurisdiction with no capital controls, easy banking, strong crypto regulation.
- **Pitfall**: Extremely expensive housing. Many nomads use serviced apartments or co-living spaces.
- Octopus card is essential from day one.
- Excellent base for managing finances across Asia.

## Mexico

### Banking
- **Best banks**: BBVA Mexico (largest, best digital platform), Banorte, Santander Mexico, Citibanamex, Banco Azteca (more accessible but basic).
- **Account opening**: Requires **Residente Temporal or Permanente** visa. Tourist visa holders cannot open accounts.
- Documents: Passport, residency visa, proof of address (utility bill/rental contract), RFC (Mexican tax ID).
- **RFC**: Can sometimes be generated at BBVA branches, but better to obtain beforehand through SAT. Digital nomads not earning in Mexico are not required to pay taxes but still need RFC for banking.
- Actinver recommended for international transfer flexibility.
- **Warning**: Intercam had US sanctions imposed in June 2025 for alleged money laundering ties -- avoid.

### Payment Apps
- **SPEI**: Mexico's real-time gross settlement system -- instant, free bank-to-bank transfers.
- **CoDi**: QR code payment system built on SPEI infrastructure.
- **DiMo**: Mobile phone number-based instant payments (newer than CoDi).
- Mercado Pago (from Mercado Libre) is widely used.
- Rappi also offers a digital wallet.

### Currency Restrictions
- MXN is freely convertible and relatively stable.
- No significant capital controls for personal amounts.
- Cash USD deposits above $4,000/month at banks trigger enhanced reporting.
- Banks must report international transfers following CNBV regulations (major fines in 2025 for non-compliance).

### Crypto Regulation
- Regulated under the **2018 Fintech Law**: Non-financial entities can operate exchanges; financial institutions generally cannot offer public crypto services.
- Virtual assets are **not legal tender**.
- **Tax**: Income tax up to 35% for individuals; VAT (16%) on services exchanged for crypto.
- Transactions over $12,500: Buyer may need to withhold 20%.
- Major 2025 AML amendment: New reporting thresholds for VASPs at transactions 210+ UMA (~$1,180).

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-residents taxed on Mexican-sourced income only.
- Tax residency: Established primary residence or center of economic interests in Mexico.
- Digital nomads on temporary residency earning exclusively from abroad: Generally not required to file Mexican taxes, but having RFC does not automatically create tax obligations.
- Withholding tax on services: 25% on gross income.
- Mexico has 50+ tax treaties.

### Moving Money
- SPEI makes domestic transfers instant and free.
- Wise supports MXN.
- Remittances from US are enormous ($60B+ annually) -- many options: Remitly, Xoom, Western Union, MoneyGram.
- ATM fees vary; BBVA and Santander tend to be lower.

### Fintech Ecosystem
- Booming: Nubank Mexico, Stori, Klar (neobanks), Clip (POS terminals), Kueski (lending).
- Mercado Pago dominates e-commerce payments.
- SPEI/CoDi/DiMo infrastructure is modern and efficient.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Mexico City $1,000-2,000/month; Oaxaca $700-1,200/month; Playa del Carmen $1,000-1,800/month.
- **Pitfall**: Getting an RFC and bank account requires residency visa -- plan ahead during visa process.
- SPEI is excellent -- once you have a Mexican bank account, transfers are instant and free.
- Cash is still needed in markets and smaller businesses.

## Brazil

### Banking

- **PIX**: Brazil's revolutionary instant payment system. Free, instant, 24/7. Used by virtually everyone.

- BRL is managed float. Not freely convertible for large amounts.

### Crypto Regulation
- Regulated market with formal VASP licensing.
- CPF required for all BRL crypto transactions (AML compliance).
- **R$35,000/month exemption** for retail capital gains still applies in 2026.
- DeCripto system used for crypto tax reporting.
- Major exchanges: Mercado Bitcoin, Foxbit, Binance Brazil.

### Taxes for Non-Residents
- Non-residents taxed at a flat **15-25%** on Brazilian-sourced income (depending on type).
- Tax residency: Permanent visa holders or temporary visa holders present 183+ days in 12-month period.

- CPF does not automatically create tax obligations -- it's just an ID number.

### Moving Money
- Wise supports BRL but note the 3.5% IOF on international transfers.
- PIX is domestic-only but incredibly efficient once you have a local account.
- Remessa Online is popular for sending/receiving international transfers.

- **Most advanced in Latin America**: Nubank (valued at ~$85B), Inter, C6 Bank.
- PIX handles more transactions than credit/debit cards combined.
- Open Finance system (Regulamento by BCB) expanding.
- Mercado Pago widely used for e-commerce.

### Nomad-Specific Tips
- Cost of living: Sao Paulo $1,000-2,000/month; Rio de Janeiro $900-1,800/month; Florianopolis $700-1,300/month.
- **Step 1**: Get a CPF. This unlocks everything (banking, SIM card, some hotels).
- **Pitfall**: The 3.5% IOF on international transfers is steep.
- PIX is a game-changer once you're set up -- fastest payment system in Latin America.
- Digital nomad visa available for remote workers.

## Universal Tips

1. **Always have 2-3 ways to access money**: International debit card + cash + Wise card as backup.
2. **Notify your bank before traveling** to avoid card blocks.
3. **Use ATMs inside banks** to reduce skimming risk.
4. **Avoid dynamic currency conversion** (DCC) -- always choose local currency at ATMs/POS.
5. **Keep records** of all international transfers for tax compliance in your home country.
6. **Tax residency is cumulative** -- track your days in each country carefully.
7. **Get local SIM cards early** -- most banking apps and payment wallets require local phone numbers.
8. **183-day rule is near-universal** -- staying under 183 days in most countries avoids triggering tax residency.
