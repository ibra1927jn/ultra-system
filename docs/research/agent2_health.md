## 1. PREVENTIVE HEALTH

### Medical Checkups by Age

**Age 25:**
- Blood work: Complete blood count (CBC), lipid panel, fasting glucose, thyroid (TSH), Vitamin D, B12, iron/ferritin. Do annually.
- Dental: Cleaning + exam every 6-12 months
- Vision: Eye exam every 2 years (annually if wearing corrective lenses)
- Skin: Self-check monthly; professional baseline mole-map if fair-skinned or heavy sun exposure
- STI: Full panel (HIV, syphilis, gonorrhea, chlamydia, Hep B/C) annually if sexually active with new partners, every 3-6 months if high risk
- Blood pressure: Every 1-2 years

**Age 30:**
- All of the above, plus:
- Diabetes screening (HbA1c) if overweight or family history
- Testicular self-exam monthly (men)
- Cervical screening/Pap smear every 3 years (women, from age 25)
- Consider baseline ECG if family history of heart disease
- Hepatitis C screening (one-time, recommended for all adults)

**Age 35:**
- All of the above, plus:
- Lipid panel annually (cholesterol becomes more critical)
- Blood pressure every year
- Consider cardiovascular risk assessment
- Prostate awareness (men, discuss with doctor if family history)
- Diabetes screening every 3 years regardless

**Age 40:**
- All of the above, plus:
- Diabetes screening every 3 years (all adults)
- Colonoscopy discussion (especially if family history; standard screening starts at 45 in most guidelines)
- Annual eye exam (presbyopia begins)
- ECG baseline if not done
- Skin cancer screening annually if high UV exposure history

### Deworming

**When needed:**
- After spending time in tropical/subtropical developing countries, especially if eating street food or walking barefoot
- Every 6-12 months if living in SEA, India, Africa, Latin America
- Symptoms: bloating, unexplained weight loss, abdominal pain, diarrhea, visible worms, fatigue, itching around anus

**OTC medications:**
- **Albendazole** 400mg single dose (most common, broad spectrum) -- OTC in most of Asia, Africa, Latin America. ~$0.50-2
- **Mebendazole** 500mg single dose or 100mg x 3 days -- OTC same regions. ~$0.50-2
- Both are WHO essential medicines and very safe
- Can buy without prescription in: Thailand, India, Vietnam, Cambodia, Philippines, Mexico, Colombia, Peru, most of Africa
- In Western countries: usually prescription-only but cheap (~$5-15)

**Protocol:** Take albendazole 400mg as a single dose on a full stomach. Repeat in 2 weeks if symptoms persist. For hookworm or strongyloides, take 400mg daily for 3 days.

## 2. DEEP MENTAL HEALTH

### Journaling Methods

**Morning Pages (Julia Cameron method):**
- Write 3 pages longhand immediately upon waking, stream of consciousness
- No editing, no reading back (initially)
- Purpose: clears mental clutter, surfaces anxieties, sparks creativity
- Cost: Notebook + pen. That's it.

**Bullet Journaling (Ryder Carroll method):**
- Rapid logging with bullets: tasks (dots), events (circles), notes (dashes)
- Monthly/weekly/daily spreads
- Migration: review and intentionally move unfinished tasks
- Good for: Combining planning with reflection
- Just need any notebook

**Gratitude Journaling:**
- Write 3 specific things you're grateful for each night
- Be specific ("the stranger who gave me directions in Medellin" not just "people")
- Research shows measurable improvement in wellbeing after 3 weeks
- Can combine with bullet journal

**Structured CBT Journaling (Thought Records):**
- Situation -> Automatic Thought -> Emotion (rate 0-100) -> Evidence For -> Evidence Against -> Balanced Thought -> Re-rate Emotion
- Most evidence-based journaling for anxiety/depression
- Free templates: search "CBT thought record worksheet PDF"

**Free/cheap apps:**
- **Day One**: Free tier (1 journal, limited entries), Premium $35/year
- **Journey**: Free tier, cross-platform, prompts included
- **Stoic**: Free mental health journal with CBT prompts, mood tracking
- **Obsidian**: Free, markdown-based, works offline, plugin ecosystem (use daily notes + templates for journaling)
- **Notion**: Free tier, build your own journaling system
- **Simple pen and paper**: Most reliable, no battery, works offline. Best option for van life.

### Self-Administered CBT/DBT

**Feeling Good by David Burns protocol:**
- The gold standard self-help CBT book
- Core technique: identify cognitive distortions (all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing, mind reading, etc.)
- Daily mood log: rate moods, identify distortions, write rational responses
- Free PDF worksheets available at feelinggood.com
- Companion workbook: "Ten Days to Self-Esteem" (structured 10-step program)

**Key CBT cognitive distortions to learn:**
1. All-or-nothing thinking
2. Overgeneralization
3. Mental filter (dwelling on negatives)
4. Disqualifying the positive
5. Jumping to conclusions (mind reading, fortune telling)
6. Magnification/minimization
7. Emotional reasoning
8. Should statements
9. Labeling
10. Personalization

**DBT Skills (4 modules):**

1. **Distress Tolerance** (surviving crises without making them worse):
   - TIPP: Temperature (cold water on face), Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Paired muscle relaxation
   - ACCEPTS: Activities, Contributing, Comparisons, Emotions (opposite), Pushing away, Thoughts, Sensations
   - Radical acceptance

2. **Emotion Regulation:**
   - ABC PLEASE: Accumulate positives, Build mastery, Cope ahead, treat PhysicaL illness, balance Eating, avoid mood-Altering substances, balance Sleep, get Exercise
   - Opposite action (do the opposite of what the unhelpful emotion urges)
   - Check the facts

3. **Mindfulness:**
   - What skills: Observe, Describe, Participate
   - How skills: Non-judgmentally, One-mindfully, Effectively

4. **Interpersonal Effectiveness:**
   - DEAR MAN: Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce, Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate
   - GIVE: Gentle, Interested, Validate, Easy manner
   - FAST: Fair, no Apologies (unnecessary), Stick to values, Truthful

### Developer Burnout

**Warning signs:**
- Dreading opening your laptop
- Cynicism about work/projects that used to excite you
- Difficulty concentrating, simple tasks feel overwhelming
- Physical symptoms: headaches, insomnia, fatigue
- Increased substance use (coffee, alcohol, cannabis)
- Social withdrawal

**Prevention:**
- Hard boundaries: No work after X time. No work on weekends (or at least one full day off)
- Pomodoro technique: 25 min work / 5 min break. After 4 rounds, 30 min break
- Physical work/exercise as counterbalance to sedentary coding
- Nature exposure: hike, swim, sit outside. The van lifestyle actually helps here
- Side projects should be DIFFERENT from work (woodworking, cooking, music, not more coding)
- Track hours actually worked -- most people overestimate

**Recovery protocol (when burned out):**
1. Take minimum 1 week fully off (no code, no Slack, no GitHub)
2. Sleep as much as your body wants
3. Exercise daily but gently (walking, swimming)
4. Reconnect with non-tech interests
5. Slowly reintroduce work with strict time limits
6. Consider changing what you work on (different project, language, domain)
7. If freelance: raise rates, reduce hours. Trading money for time is the burnout cure.

**Digital detox strategies:**
- Phone grayscale mode (reduces dopamine pull)
- Delete social media apps, use only via browser
- No screens 1 hour before bed
- "Airplane mode mornings" -- first hour of the day screen-free
- Leave phone in the van when going for walks
- Use website blockers: Cold Turkey, Freedom, LeechBlock

## 4. SUBSTANCES & ADDICTION

### Coffee Optimization

**Caffeine science:**
- Half-life: 5-6 hours (varies by genetics -- CYP1A2 gene)
- Quarter-life: 10-12 hours (still 25% in your system)
- **Rule: No caffeine after 2 PM** for good sleep (10+ hours before bedtime)

**Optimal timing (cortisol cycle):**
- Cortisol peaks: 8-9 AM, 12-1 PM, 5:30-6:30 PM
- Best caffeine windows: **9:30-11:30 AM** and **1:30-3:00 PM** (between cortisol peaks)
- Never drink coffee within 90 minutes of waking -- cortisol is already doing the job
- Hydrate first: 500ml water upon waking, then coffee after 90 min

**L-Theanine stacking:**
- 100-200mg L-Theanine with each coffee
- Eliminates jitters and anxiety while preserving focus
- Creates "calm alertness"
- Available as supplement, naturally present in green tea
- Cheap: $10-15 for 60+ capsules, available on iHerb (ships worldwide)

**Caffeine tolerance reset protocol:**
- Gradual: Reduce by 25% every 3 days over 2 weeks, then stay at zero for 7-10 days
- Cold turkey: Expect 2-4 days of headaches, fatigue. Take ibuprofen for headaches.
- After reset, caffeine will be dramatically more effective at lower doses
- Recommended reset every 2-3 months

**Low-pressure/dry cultures:**

- India (many regions -- alcohol stigmatized)

**Health thresholds:**
- Low risk: Up to 14 units/week (men and women), spread over 3+ days
- 1 unit = 10ml pure alcohol = half pint beer, small glass wine, single spirit
- Beyond 14 units/week: measurable increase in health risks
- Recent research suggests NO amount is truly "safe" -- it's dose-dependent risk
- For a physical worker/athlete: alcohol impairs recovery, sleep quality, and hydration significantly

- **Pulse oximeter** ($10-20 -- monitors blood oxygen, invaluable at altitude and for illness)

**Medications to carry:**
- Ibuprofen 200mg + Paracetamol/Acetaminophen 500mg
- Antihistamine (cetirizine or diphenhydramine)
- Anti-diarrheal (loperamide/Imodium)
- Oral Rehydration Salts

- Sunburn relief (aloe vera gel)
- Motion sickness tablets (meclizine or dimenhydrinate)
- Melatonin (for jet lag / sleep schedule resets)

**EpiPen considerations:** If you have known severe allergies, carry 2 EpiPens. They're expensive ($300+ in the US) but cheaper alternatives exist: Jext in Europe, generic epinephrine in India/SEA ($10-30). Keep them temperature-controlled (they degrade in heat above 25C -- a challenge in a van). Consider an insulated case.

### Essential Medications by Region

**Antibiotics OTC (no prescription) in:**
- Most of Southeast Asia (Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Philippines)
- India, Nepal, Bangladesh

**Key medications to stock up on while in these countries:**
- **Ciprofloxacin 500mg** (traveler's diarrhea, UTI) -- 10 tablets
- **Azithromycin 500mg** (respiratory infections, traveler's diarrhea backup, STIs) -- 6 tablets
- **Amoxicillin 500mg** (dental infections, ear infections, respiratory) -- 20 tablets
- **Metronidazole 400mg** (giardia, amoebic dysentery, dental abscess) -- 20 tablets

- **Albendazole 400mg** (deworming) -- 4 tablets

**IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER:** Self-treating with antibiotics is risky. Learn when to use each one. Misuse promotes antibiotic resistance. When possible, see a doctor. These are for situations where medical care is inaccessible.

### Malaria Prophylaxis

| Drug | Cost/week | Side Effects | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Doxycycline** | $0.50-2 | Sun sensitivity (big problem for outdoor life!), GI upset, yeast infections | Cheapest. Take daily. Also protects against some other infections. |
| **Malarone (atovaquone/proguanil)** | $5-15 (generic much cheaper in Asia/Africa: $1-3) | Few side effects, well-tolerated | Best tolerated. Take daily. Start 1-2 days before, continue 7 days after. |
| **Mefloquine (Lariam)** | $3-8 | Vivid dreams, anxiety, depression, psychosis (rare but serious) | Weekly dosing (convenient). NOT recommended if history of depression/anxiety. |

**Recommendation:** Malarone (generic) bought in Asia/Africa is best value. If budget is very tight and you don't mind sun sensitivity, doxycycline. Avoid mefloquine if you have any mental health concerns.

**When needed:** Only in active malaria zones. NOT needed in most of SEA (mainly border areas, rural), NOT needed in most cities. Check CDC malaria map. Mainly needed for: Sub-Saharan Africa, parts of India, Papua New Guinea, Solomon Islands, rural Amazon.

### Traveler's Diarrhea Protocol

**ORS Recipe (WHO formula, make your own):**
- 1 liter clean/boiled water
- 6 teaspoons sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- Mix and sip throughout the day. This saves lives.

**Escalation protocol:**
1. **Mild** (loose stools, no fever): ORS, bland diet (BRAT: bananas, rice, applesauce, toast), wait it out. Usually resolves in 1-3 days.
2. **Moderate** (frequent watery stools, cramps, no blood): Add loperamide (Imodium) 4mg initially then 2mg after each loose stool (max 16mg/day). Continue ORS aggressively.
3. **Severe / bacterial** (fever >38.5C, blood in stool, OR debilitating for >48 hours): **Ciprofloxacin 500mg twice daily for 3 days** OR **Azithromycin 500mg once daily for 3 days** (preferred in SEA due to cipro resistance). Stop loperamide if blood in stool.
4. **Seek medical help if:** Blood in stool + high fever, symptoms >5 days despite antibiotics, signs of severe dehydration (no urination for 12+ hours, confusion, extreme weakness), suspected cholera (rice-water stools, massive volume).

**Giardia (foul-smelling, sulfurous burps, bloating, chronic):** Metronidazole 250mg three times daily for 5-7 days. Or Tinidazole 2g single dose.

### Laundry

**Hand washing technique:**
1. Fill bucket/sink with warm water + small amount of detergent or Dr. Bronner's castile soap
2. Agitate clothes for 3-5 minutes, focus on pits/collar/crotch
3. Soak 15-30 minutes for heavily soiled items
4. Rinse in clean water (2 rinses minimum)
5. Wring out by twisting in a towel (roll clothes in a dry towel, step on it -- extracts far more water than hand-wringing)

## 7. HEALTH RISKS BY REGION

### Southeast Asia (Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Philippines, Indonesia, Malaysia)

**Diseases:**
- **Dengue**: Present EVERYWHERE in SEA. No prophylaxis. Prevent mosquito bites (day-biting Aedes mosquitoes). Symptoms: high fever, severe headache, pain behind eyes, joint pain, rash. Avoid aspirin/ibuprofen (increases bleeding risk) -- use only paracetamol. Seek medical help.
- **Malaria**: Risk mainly in rural/border/jungle areas (Cambodia-Myanmar-Laos borders, rural Philippines, Kalimantan/Papua Indonesia). NOT in cities, major tourist areas, or most of Thailand. Use prophylaxis only if going to specific risk areas.
- **Japanese Encephalitis**: Risk in rural rice-growing areas. Vaccine recommended if spending extended time in rural SEA.
- **Rabies**: Stray dogs everywhere. Monkeys in temples. Avoid touching animals. Get pre-exposure vaccine.

**Water safety:** Do NOT drink tap water anywhere in SEA. Use sealed bottled water or filtered water. Ice in restaurants is usually factory-made (hollow cylinder shape) and safe. Street food is generally safe if freshly cooked and hot.

**Other hazards:**
- Heat stroke: Acclimatize slowly, drink 3-4L water daily, recognize signs (confusion, hot dry skin, no sweating)
- Jellyfish: Box jellyfish in Thai Gulf waters (Oct-May especially). Vinegar for stings, NOT fresh water.
- Rabid dogs: Especially Bali, rural Thailand, Cambodia. Do NOT pet strays.
- Motorbike accidents: #1 killer of tourists in SEA. Wear helmets, don't drink and ride.
- Air pollution: Burning season in northern Thailand/Myanmar (Feb-Apr). Check AQI app.

### India / Nepal

**Key risks:**
- **"Delhi belly"**: Almost inevitable. Build up slowly, eat freshly cooked food, avoid salads/raw vegetables initially. Carry ciprofloxacin and ORS.
- **Typhoid**: Get vaccinated before visiting India. Common in contaminated food/water.
- **Altitude**: Nepal treks above 2,500m risk altitude sickness. Acclimatize (ascend max 500m/day above 3,000m), carry Diamox (acetazolamide 125-250mg twice daily for prevention, available OTC in Nepal pharmacies for ~$0.10/tablet).
- **Air pollution**: Delhi, Lahore, Kolkata among world's worst. Wear N95/KN95 mask. Check AQI daily. Consider limiting time in mega-cities during winter (worst pollution Nov-Feb).
- **Rabies**: Very high risk. Stray dogs everywhere. Monkeys aggressive at temples. Pre-exposure vaccine ESSENTIAL.
- **Water**: NEVER drink tap water. Even in nice restaurants, confirm water is filtered/bottled.
- **Hepatitis A & E**: Fecal-oral route through contaminated food/water. Hep A vaccine essential. No vaccine for Hep E -- prevent through food/water hygiene.

### Latin America

**Key risks:**
- **Altitude sickness**: Bogota (2,640m), Quito (2,850m), Cusco (3,400m), La Paz (3,640m), Mexico City (2,240m -- mild). Same Diamox protocol as Nepal. Coca tea (mate de coca) is traditional and mildly helpful in Andean countries.
- **Mosquito-borne diseases**: Dengue (widespread, especially below 2,000m), Zika (risk for pregnant women), Chikungunya (severe joint pain for weeks/months). Use DEET 30%+ repellent, long sleeves at dawn/dusk.
- **Chagas disease**: Transmitted by triatomid "kissing bugs" in rural areas, especially thatched/adobe housing. Risk mainly for long-term rural stays in Bolivia, Paraguay, Argentina, Central America. Use mosquito net in rural areas.
- **Water**: Varies. Generally safe in major cities of Chile, Argentina, Uruguay, Costa Rica, Mexico City. NOT safe in rural areas, Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador, Guatemala, Honduras.
- **Sun**: Extremely intense at altitude. Quito/Bogota UV index can hit 11+ (extreme). The equatorial sun at 3,000m+ altitude is brutal. SPF 50+ mandatory.

### Africa

**Key risks:**
- **Malaria**: The big one. Sub-Saharan Africa has the most dangerous malaria (P. falciparum). Prophylaxis is ESSENTIAL in most of sub-Saharan Africa (except high-altitude areas like Nairobi, Addis Ababa). Use insecticide-treated bed net, DEET repellent, and take prophylaxis consistently.
- **Yellow Fever**: Vaccine certificate REQUIRED for entry to many African countries (and required for entry to other countries AFTER visiting Africa). Get the vaccine.
- **Bilharzia (Schistosomiasis)**: Freshwater parasites in lakes and rivers, especially Lake Malawi, Lake Victoria, Nile tributaries. Do NOT swim in fresh water in East/Central/West Africa. If exposed, get tested 6-12 weeks later (blood test), treat with praziquantel.
- **Water**: Not safe in most of Africa. Boil, filter, or use bottled.
- **Sun**: Intense, especially in southern Africa and at altitude (Kenya, Ethiopia).
- **HIV prevalence**: Very high in southern/eastern Africa (Eswatini 27%, South Africa 19%, Botswana 21%). Exercise extreme caution.

### Australia / New Zealand

**Key risks:**
- **UV radiation**: Australia and NZ have the HIGHEST UV levels in the world due to ozone hole and clear skies. UV index routinely hits 12-14+ in summer. Skin cancer rates highest globally. Slip (on a shirt), Slop (on sunscreen), Slap (on a hat), Seek (shade), Slide (on sunglasses). This is not optional.
- **Spiders (Australia)**: Funnel-web spider (Sydney area -- aggressive, can be fatal, seek emergency help immediately), Redback spider (nationwide -- painful but rarely fatal, apply ice, seek medical help). Shake out shoes and clothes left outside.
- **Snakes (Australia)**: Eastern brown, taipan, tiger snake -- among world's most venomous. If bitten: pressure immobilization bandage (wrap firmly from bite to whole limb), do NOT cut/suck, do NOT wash bite site (doctors need to identify venom), call 000 emergency.
- **Marine stingers (Australia)**: Box jellyfish (Oct-May, tropical QLD/NT coast), Irukandji jellyfish (tiny, extremely dangerous). Stinger nets at beaches, wear stinger suits for swimming. Blue-ringed octopus: DO NOT touch -- no effective antivenom, can be fatal.
- **New Zealand**: Relatively safe. No snakes. Main hazards: UV exposure, hypothermia in mountains (weather changes rapidly), sandflies (South Island west coast -- extremely annoying bites, use DEET).
- **Water**: Tap water safe throughout both countries.

### Middle East / North Africa

**Key risks:**
- **Extreme heat**: Summer temperatures 40-50C in Gulf states, North Africa, Iran. Risk of heat stroke, severe dehydration. Drink 4-6L water daily in summer. Avoid outdoor exertion 11am-4pm.
- **Dehydration**: The #1 health risk. Dry air means you lose water without noticing sweat. Urine should be light yellow -- if dark, drink more.
- **Sand flies (Leishmaniasis)**: Present in rural areas of Middle East, North Africa. Causes skin sores or internal organ infection. Use DEET, sleep with fine-mesh net. Treatable but unpleasant.
- **Water**: Tap water NOT safe in Morocco, Egypt, Jordan (most of the region). Safe in Israel, UAE (desalinated), Oman (mostly).
- **Food safety**: Eat freshly cooked food. Avoid uncooked vegetables in Morocco, Egypt.
- **Respiratory**: Dust storms can be severe. Carry a face mask.

### Europe

**Key risks:**
- **Tick-borne diseases**:
  - **Lyme disease**: Present in most of Europe, especially Central Europe, Scandinavia, UK. Caused by Ixodes ticks in forests/grasslands. Check your body after outdoor activities. Look for "bull's eye" rash (erythema migrans). Early treatment with doxycycline is curative. Untreated can cause chronic problems.
  - **Tick-borne encephalitis (TBE)**: Central/Eastern Europe, Scandinavia, Baltics. Vaccine available and recommended if spending time in forests in Austria, Czech Republic, Germany, Sweden, Finland, Baltics. No specific treatment once infected.
- **Air pollution**: Eastern Europe (Poland, Bulgaria, North Macedonia, Bosnia) has severe winter air pollution from coal heating. Check AQI. Wear masks in bad periods.
- **Winter cold**: Hypothermia risk for van dwellers! Below 0C nights common Oct-Apr in Central/Northern Europe. Solutions: diesel heater (Webasto/Espar or Chinese clone $150-300), proper sleeping bag rated to -10C or lower, insulate the van (window covers, floor insulation), wool/merino base layers. Never use propane heaters in enclosed van (carbon monoxide risk) -- if you must, use a CO detector.
- **Water**: Tap water safe in almost all of Western/Northern Europe. Some Eastern European countries have variable quality (check locally).
- **Healthcare access**: EHIC/GHIC card covers EU/EEA citizens for emergency care. Non-EU travelers should have travel insurance.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS & PRIORITY ACTIONS

1. **Get vaccinated** before leaving: Hep A+B, Typhoid, pre-exposure Rabies (ideally in Thailand for cheapest). These are the highest-ROI health investments.
2. **Build the van first aid kit** from the advanced list above. Stock up on OTC medications when in SEA or Latin America.
3. **SPF 50+ sunscreen every single day** -- especially in Australia/NZ. Non-negotiable.
4. **Carry ORS packets everywhere**. Dehydration from diarrhea, heat, or altitude is the most common health emergency for travelers.
5. **Mental health routine**: Daily journaling (even 5 min), one CCI workbook, weekly video call with a close friend. Loneliness is the silent killer of nomad life.
6. **Dental care**: Get a full checkup + cleaning in Thailand or Mexico every 6-12 months. Carry an electric toothbrush and floss daily.
7. **Blood work annually**: Do it in Thailand or India for $15-45. Track your trends.
8. **Travel insurance**: Always have it. World Nomads, SafetyWing ($45/month), or Genki ($35-60/month for EU). Never skip this.
9. **Coffee after 9:30am, none after 2pm. Stack with L-theanine.** Small optimization, big impact on sleep and focus.
10. **Deworm every 6-12 months** if in developing countries. Albendazole 400mg, single dose, OTC.
